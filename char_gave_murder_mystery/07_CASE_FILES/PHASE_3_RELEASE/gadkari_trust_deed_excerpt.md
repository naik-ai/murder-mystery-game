## RECITALS

WHEREAS the Founder, Shri Vishwanath Damodar Gadkari, son of late Shri Damodar Gadkari, resident of Gadkari Wada, Village Talegaon, Taluka Satara, District Satara, being the owner in possession of various properties hereinafter described, and

WHEREAS the Founder is desirous of creating a trust for religious, charitable, and family purposes for the benefit of the Devasthan (temple) and his family members and descendants, and

WHEREAS the Founder wishes to ensure that certain properties shall be held in trust for specific purposes and beneficiaries as described herein,

NOW THIS DEED WITNESSETH as follows:

## ARTICLE II: BENEFICIARIES

### Part A: Temple Beneficiaries
[Temple trust beneficiaries - not relevant to excerpt]

### Part B: Family Beneficiaries

**Primary Beneficiaries (in order of succession):**

1. Shri Vishwanath D. Gadkari (Founder) - during his lifetime
2. After Founder, his legitimate son(s) in order of birth
3. After sons, legitimate grandson(s) in order of birth
4. [Standard succession provisions - continues for several generations]

**Contingent Beneficiaries:**

In the event that any child or descendant of the Founder, whether legitimate or otherwise, is born and acknowledged by the Founder during his lifetime or identified by DNA or blood testing after his death, such child shall be entitled to:

- Equal share in Schedule B properties with other beneficiaries of same generation
- Right of residence at Gadkari Wada
- Monthly maintenance from trust income (amount to be determined by trustees)

**Provision for Minor Dependents:**

> Any minor child of the Founder, whether legitimate or acknowledged, shall have the right to education, maintenance, and wedding expenses from trust funds. The trustees shall ensure such children are protected regardless of circumstances of birth.

## ARTICLE III: TRUSTEES

**Initial Trustees:**
1. Shri Vishwanath D. Gadkari (Founder - Managing Trustee)
2. Shri Ramchandra P. Bhave (Temple Priest)
3. Shri [Name illegible in copy] (Village Elder)

**Succession of Trusteeship:**
After Founder, the eldest living male descendant shall become Managing Trustee. Temple priest position shall carry automatic trusteeship.

---

## ARTICLE IV: TRUST PURPOSES

The properties in Schedule B shall be managed for:

1. Maintenance of temple and religious activities
2. Education of beneficiaries
3. Welfare of acknowledged descendants regardless of legitimacy
4. Charitable distribution to village poor (10% of annual income)

---

## ARTICLE V: RESTRICTIONS

**5.1 Alienation Restrictions:**

Properties in Schedule B shall NOT be sold, mortgaged, or transferred outside the trust except:
- With unanimous consent of all living beneficiaries, OR
- By order of competent court

**5.2 Beneficiary Protection:**

No beneficiary shall be removed from trust benefits except:
- By death, OR
- By voluntary written renunciation, OR
- By order of competent court for proven misconduct

**5.3 Amendment Restrictions:**

This trust deed may be amended only for:
- Addition of newly acknowledged beneficiaries
- Administrative procedural changes
- NOT for removal of existing beneficiaries without their consent

---

## ARTICLE VI: SPECIAL PROVISIONS

### Provision 6.3 (Relevant Excerpt):

> In the event that any minor beneficiary dies before attaining majority, their share shall pass to their legal heirs. If no legal heirs exist, the share shall be held in trust for future minor beneficiaries or, in absence thereof, shall be utilized for charitable purposes approved by the temple committee.

### Provision 6.5:

> If dispute arises regarding beneficiary status, the matter shall be decided by a committee comprising the temple priest, village Sarpanch, and one elder nominated by the Founder's family. DNA testing or other scientific methods may be employed where available and appropriate.

## NOTE FROM TEMPLE COMMITTEE

*Attached to this document:*

> This deed was stored in temple records since 1962. It was examined by the current priest, Pandit Venkatesh, at the request of the Gadkari family in 1987 when question arose about certain children from the Vasti settlement.
>
> The question was whether these children were covered under Article II Part B of this deed.
>
> Temple committee meeting was held. No formal decision was recorded.
>
> Shortly thereafter, the children in question (twin girls, age 8) died in drowning incident at Korde Tal.
>
> No further discussion has occurred on this matter since 1988.
>
> We provide this excerpt for investigation purposes only. Temple takes no position on interpretation of the deed.
